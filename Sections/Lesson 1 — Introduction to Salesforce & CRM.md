# Lesson 1 — Introduction to Salesforce & CRM

## Lesson Summary

This lesson introduces **Salesforce** and **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)**. It explains how Salesforce helps businesses manage customer relationships and improve profitability. The lesson also provides an overview of major Salesforce CRM components and explains why Salesforce became popular.

---

## Key Points

- **Salesforce** is a company founded by **Marc Benioff**
- One of its main products is **Salesforce CRM**
- **CRM** stands for **Customer Relationship Management**
- Salesforce CRM helps manage customer interactions and improve business outcomes
- Major Salesforce areas:
    - **Sales**
    - **Service**
    - **Marketing**
    - **Chatter**
    - **Analytics**
    - **Force.com**
- Salesforce is popular because it is:
    - **Cloud-based**
    - Uses **Pay-as-you-go pricing**
    - Offers **Reporting & Analytics**

---

## Detailed Notes

### What is Salesforce?

- Salesforce is a **cloud-based software company**.
- Its most recognized product is **Salesforce CRM**.
- Businesses use Salesforce to manage customer data and interactions.

---

### What is CRM?

**CRM (Customer Relationship Management)** is software that helps businesses:

- Track customer information
- Maintain relationships with customers
- Manage potential customers (leads)
- Improve profitability

---

### Salesforce CRM Components

#### 1. Sales Cloud

**Purpose:**
- Improve sales productivity
- Manage leads and opportunities
- Help teams close deals

**Key Idea:**
- No hardware or software installation required.

---

#### 2. Service Cloud

**Purpose:**
- Improve customer support

**Features:**
- Call center support
- Self-service portals
- Flexible customer service

---

#### 3. Marketing Cloud

**Purpose:**
- Deliver personalized customer experiences

**Channels:**
- Email
- Mobile
- Social media
- Web

---

#### 4. Chatter

**Purpose:**
- Internal collaboration

**Features:**
- Employee communication
- File sharing
- Knowledge sharing

---

#### 5. Analytics

**Purpose:**
- Collect and analyze business data

**Benefits:**
- Unified customer view
- Faster decision making

---

#### 6. Force.com

**Purpose:**
- Build applications

**Methods:**
- **Clicks (Low-code)**
- **Code (Development)**

---

### Why Salesforce is Popular?

#### Cloud-Based
- No infrastructure maintenance
- Accessible online

#### Pay-As-You-Go Model
Supports:
- Small businesses
- Medium organizations
- Large enterprises

#### Reporting & Analysis
Provides:
- Dashboards
- Reports
- Real-time business insights
